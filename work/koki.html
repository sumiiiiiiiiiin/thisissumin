PROBLEM

While working as a server at a Korean restaurant in Toronto, I noticed that many customers struggled to understand the menu.

Dish names were unfamiliar, and menu information was often unclear. As Korean cuisine often involves multiple customization options, customers needed extra time to decide on additional options, which led to delays and confusion.

This experience led me to explore how UX design could improve menu clarity and support better decision-making.

Challenge

How might we help customers quickly understand unfamiliar Korean dishes and order with confidence?

RESEARCH

While working as a server at a Korean restaurant, I served around 30 tables per shift. I closely observed how customers interacted with the menu and I noticed three key patterns through daily observations for a week.

63%

of customers struggled to decide what to order.

Most common questions from customers

Is this spicy?

How big is the portion?

Are there any vegan options?

What’s in this dish?

How do I eat this?

AI ASSISTANCE

Based on my research, I used Claude AI to explore UX structure ideas. Through this process, I gained three key outputs.

💬 Conversational UX Direction
A shift from traditional filter-based navigation to a more intuitive, conversational AI chatbot approach.

⏰ Efficient Content Generation
AI-generated dish descriptions eliminated the need for manual research, allowing faster and more consistent content creation.

📝 Initial High-Fidelity Concept
AI produced an initial high-fidelity concept reflecting key user needs, which I further refined to improve clarity and usability.

SOLUTION

AI-Powered Menu Assistant

Introduced a chat-based assistant to help users explore the menu and receive personalized recommendations in a conversational way.

Improved Information Structure & Brand Identity

Removed redundant information and applied a consistent visual identity to create a clearer and more cohesive experience.

claude

Immediate Visual Feedback

Designed option selections to provide instant feedback, helping users confirm their choices and reduce uncertainty.

Guided Navigation Flow

Placed every button to go next step in the bottom right corner to maintain flow and help users move through the process without interruption.

claude

Transparent & Editable Cart Experience

Displayed selected options and special requests directly within the cart, allowing users to review and edit their order without leaving the page, reducing friction and improving control.

claude

Enhanced Information Visibility with Smart Tags

Used tags for key attributes such as spice level, vegan options, and portion size, along with visual indicators to help users quickly understand each dish at a glance.
