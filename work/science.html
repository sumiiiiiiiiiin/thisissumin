PROBLEM

📌   The client wanted maximum information visibility

📌   Users needed simple and fast schedule navigation

Challenge

Design a structure that presents a high volume of information clearly across desktop, tablet, and mobile without overwhelming users.

RESEARCH

The festival featured participation from a diverse audience across all age groups, including elementary, middle, and high schools, universities as well as science organizations. About two weeks, the festival offered more than 50 diverse events.

According to last year’s feedback, 72% of users struggled to navigate the schedule page

Children

Got distracted or lost in long lists of events

👧

Teenagers

Missed sessions caused by forgotten dates/times and overlapping events.

👩

Parents

Struggled to identify age-appropriate events and access detailed information.

👪

SOLUTION

Calendar Layout

The complex schedule list/table was replaced with a familiar calendar view, allowing users to quickly identify dates and weekdays, making events easier to remember.

Progressive Disclosure

The calendar shows only key event keywords, with full details available on a separate page. Direct access buttons within schedule items reduce unnecessary interaction steps.

Visual Support & Clear Categorization

To improve readability for children and teens, images were added for each event, text was reduced, and different colors were used to clearly distinguish age groups.

Save features

Quick access to saved information allows users to recall important details and make informed decisions about which events to prioritize.
